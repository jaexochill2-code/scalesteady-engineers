## Part I: Standard Service Terms

The following five sections establish the foundational legal framework governing all client engagements with ScaleSteady. These terms are consistent with industry-standard practice for professional marketing services and outbound infrastructure engagements.

---

### Section 1 — Scope of Services

**1.1 Defined Deliverables**

ScaleSteady agrees to provide the services explicitly described in the Client's accepted proposal or onboarding agreement (the "Scope of Work" or "SOW"). Services not itemized in the SOW are outside the scope of this engagement and will not be performed without a written addendum executed by both parties.

**1.2 Service Boundaries**

ScaleSteady is an outbound infrastructure company. Our engagement covers the construction, configuration, deployment, and ongoing management of the Client's outbound email system. We do not provide inbound marketing, paid media management, website development, or sales training unless separately agreed to in writing.

**1.3 Campaign Infrastructure Components**

Unless otherwise specified in writing, a standard ScaleSteady engagement includes the following deliverables:

- Deployment of dedicated sending domains and warmed email inboxes, configured to send a minimum of **20,000 emails** per campaign cycle
- Sourcing and delivery of a minimum of **10,000 verified and cleaned email addresses** drawn from the Client's target market
- Development of **two (2) distinct marketing angles**, each tailored to a segment of the Client's ideal customer profile
- Creation of **three (3) follow-up email sequences**, written to draw continued responses from prospects who did not reply to the initial outreach
- Lead list verification and cleaning, including bounce rate reduction through email validation protocols
- Inbox management, including monitoring of interested reply threads and facilitation of an initial response to qualified leads
- Coordination of follow-up on prospective meetings on the Client's behalf

**1.4 Scope Modifications**

Any request to expand, modify, or reduce the defined Scope of Work must be submitted in writing. ScaleSteady will assess the request and issue a Change Order document. No additional work shall commence until the Change Order is signed by both parties. ScaleSteady shall not be responsible for delays or outcomes arising from scope modifications that are requested but not formally authorized.

### Section 2 — Payment Terms and Pass-Through Cost Model

**2.1 Infrastructure Investment**

The Client agrees to pay a one-time infrastructure investment of **five hundred dollars (US $500)** at the commencement of services (the "Infrastructure Fee"). This fee is remitted in full to third-party vendors on the Client's behalf, including domain registrars, email data providers, and inbox warm-up platforms. ScaleSteady does not retain any portion of this fee as revenue or markup. A full itemized breakdown of vendor allocations will be provided at the time of invoicing.

**2.2 Pass-Through Cost Definition**

The Infrastructure Fee constitutes a "Pass-Through Cost." A Pass-Through Cost is a third-party expenditure made by ScaleSteady on the Client's behalf as a procuring agent. Pass-Through Costs are incurred immediately upon the placement of vendor orders and are **non-refundable once vendor purchases have been executed**. The specific refund conditions are addressed in full in Section 10.

**2.3 Agency Fee Deferral**

ScaleSteady charges **zero dollars ($0) in agency fees, labor fees, or management fees** during the campaign's initial phase. ScaleSteady absorbs all costs associated with personnel, platform licensing, copywriting, and campaign management until the Revenue Milestone defined in Section 6 is achieved, or until the Performance Period defined in Section 7 concludes. ScaleSteady operates this way because the company's financial recovery depends entirely on the Client's commercial success. There is no revenue otherwise.

**2.4 Late Payment**

If the Client elects the ongoing retainer option described in Section 9 and a monthly invoice becomes more than fourteen (14) days past due, ScaleSteady reserves the right to suspend active campaign services until the outstanding balance is resolved. Suspension does not extinguish the Client's payment obligation.

### Section 3 — Intellectual Property and Asset Ownership

**3.1 Client Ownership of Campaign Assets**

All domain names, email inboxes, lead lists, and outbound email sequences developed specifically for the Client under this engagement are the **sole property of the Client** from the moment of purchase or creation. ScaleSteady acts as the authorized technical agent managing these assets during the active campaign period. It does not hold title, equity, or licensing rights over any Client-specific campaign asset.

**3.2 ScaleSteady Proprietary Materials**

ScaleSteady retains exclusive ownership of all proprietary tools, internal automation systems, deliverability frameworks, campaign management methodologies, and operational playbooks used in the delivery of services. The Client acquires no rights, licenses, or interests in ScaleSteady's internal systems, software, or intellectual property as a result of this engagement. Deliverables provided to the Client are distinct from the methods and systems used to produce them.

**3.3 Content License During Engagement**

During the active campaign period, the Client grants ScaleSteady a limited, non-exclusive, revocable license to use the Client's name, brand, logo, products, and service descriptions solely for the purpose of constructing and deploying outbound campaign materials on the Client's behalf.

**3.4 Asset Transfer Upon Termination**

Upon termination of services for any reason, ScaleSteady will facilitate the orderly transfer of all Client-owned digital assets to the Client within ten (10) business days, including domain registrar credentials, inbox login credentials, and lead list exports in a portable format. All ScaleSteady access privileges to Client-registered accounts will be permanently revoked upon completion of the transfer.

**4.1 Scope of Confidential Information**

Both parties acknowledge that, in the course of this engagement, each may be exposed to information that is proprietary, commercially sensitive, or otherwise not intended for public disclosure. "Confidential Information" includes but is not limited to: business strategies, pricing structures, customer lists, campaign performance data, proprietary methods, software tools, financial projections, and any information marked or communicated as confidential.

**4.2 Mutual Obligation of Non-Disclosure**

Each party agrees to: (a) hold the other party's Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) not disclose such information to any third party without prior written consent; and (c) use such information solely for the purposes of fulfilling obligations under this Agreement.

**4.3 Exceptions**

Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that: (a) is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement; (b) was rightfully known to the receiving party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the receiving party without reference to the Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or valid court order.

**4.4 Duration**

Confidentiality obligations survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

### Section 5 — Term, Termination, and Offboarding

**5.1 Initial Campaign Term**

The initial engagement commences on the date of the Client's acceptance of this Agreement and continues until: (a) the Revenue Milestone is achieved; (b) the sixty (60)-day Performance Period concludes; or (c) the Agreement is terminated pursuant to the provisions below.

**5.2 Termination by Client**

The Client may terminate this Agreement at any time by providing written notice to ScaleSteady. Upon receipt of termination notice:

- All campaign activity will cease within five (5) business days
- The Infrastructure Fee for vendor purchases already executed remains non-refundable
- ScaleSteady's deferred labor fee obligation is discharged in full. No labor fees will be invoiced.
- All Client-owned assets will be transferred pursuant to Section 3.4

**5.3 Termination by ScaleSteady**

ScaleSteady reserves the right to terminate this Agreement with seven (7) business days' written notice if: (a) the Client materially breaches any provision of this Agreement and fails to cure the breach within five (5) business days of notice; (b) the Client engages in conduct that constitutes or is reasonably likely to constitute a violation of applicable email regulations (CAN-SPAM, GDPR, CASL, or equivalent); or (c) continued service would require ScaleSteady to violate applicable law.

**5.4 Effect of Termination**

Termination does not extinguish obligations that accrued prior to the effective date of termination, including any outstanding payments for services rendered. Sections 3, 4, 8, 10, 11, and 12 survive termination indefinitely.

## Part II: ScaleSteady-Specific Terms

The following sections govern the unique commercial and operational terms specific to ScaleSteady's performance-deferred, pass-through cost model. These provisions were written to give the Client a complete and honest picture of how this arrangement works, what ScaleSteady commits to, and what the Client is responsible for.

---

### Section 6 — Revenue Milestone and Performance Trigger

**6.1 Definition of Revenue Milestone**

The "Revenue Milestone" is defined as five thousand dollars (US $5,000) in new, cleared revenue received by the Client from one or more customers who were sourced through ScaleSteady's outbound campaign and who meet the attribution criteria defined in Section 6.2. Revenue from a single customer may count toward the Milestone in full, or cumulatively across multiple customers, until the $5,000 threshold is reached.

**6.2 Attribution Standard**

Revenue is deemed attributable to the campaign if the Client's new customer meets both of the following conditions: (a) the customer was either identified from the verified lead list sourced by ScaleSteady, or was engaged through a ScaleSteady-managed email sequence or follow-up; and (b) the customer did not have an existing active commercial relationship with the Client at the time the initial outreach was sent.

**6.3 Verification**

The Client shall notify ScaleSteady in writing within five (5) business days of receiving cleared funds that satisfy or contribute to the Revenue Milestone. Verification may include, at the Client's election, a bank statement, CRM record, invoice, or any other document reasonably evidencing cleared revenue. ScaleSteady relies on the Client's honest and timely self-reporting.

**6.4 Milestone Trigger**

Upon verified achievement of the Revenue Milestone, the Client's right to elect either the Scale or Walk option (as described in Section 9) becomes active. No labor fees are assessed regardless of which option the Client selects.

---

### Section 7 — Performance Period, Deferred Labor, and Downside Protection

**7.1 Performance Period**

The "Performance Period" begins on the date ScaleSteady launches the Client's first email sequence and continues for **sixty (60) calendar days**, or until the Revenue Milestone is achieved, whichever occurs first.

**7.2 ScaleSteady's Labor Commitment**

ScaleSteady commits to providing the full scope of services defined in Section 1.3 throughout the entirety of the Performance Period at no charge to the Client. All labor, management, copywriting, inbox monitoring, and optimization work performed during this period is absorbed by ScaleSteady as a condition of this Agreement.

**7.3 Outcome If Milestone Is Not Achieved**

If the Revenue Milestone is not achieved within the Performance Period, the following applies:

- ScaleSteady waives all claims to labor compensation for work performed during the Performance Period
- The Client retains full ownership of all campaign infrastructure assets: domains, warmed inboxes, verified lead lists, and email sequences
- No further fees of any nature are owed to ScaleSteady beyond the Infrastructure Fee paid at commencement

The Client's maximum financial exposure under this Agreement is five hundred dollars ($500), regardless of campaign outcome.

**7.4 No Guarantee of Commercial Outcome**

ScaleSteady warrants delivery of the infrastructure and campaign assets defined in Section 1.3. ScaleSteady does not warrant, represent, or guarantee any specific commercial outcome, including but not limited to: a specific number of replies, booked meetings, closed deals, or dollar amount of revenue generated.

Commercial outcomes depend on variables ScaleSteady does not control: the Client's sales process, the quality of the Client's offer and pricing, the capability of the Client's sales team, prevailing market conditions, and the responsiveness of the target audience. ScaleSteady has a direct financial interest in campaign performance — the company recovers its costs only when the Client closes revenue. That interest shapes how ScaleSteady works. It does not convert into a contractual guarantee.

**7.5 Past Performance Disclaimer**

Any case studies, results references, or historical performance data shared by ScaleSteady in proposals, presentations, or marketing materials are provided for illustrative purposes only. Past performance in prior client engagements does not guarantee equivalent results in any current or future engagement. Each campaign operates within its own market context, and outcomes will vary.

### Section 8 — Sole Operational Control of Infrastructure

**8.1 ScaleSteady's Technical Authority**

For the duration of the active campaign, ScaleSteady shall retain **sole operational control** over all sending infrastructure, including: domain configuration, DNS records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), inbox warm-up schedules, sending sequences, automation workflows, and email delivery platforms.

**8.2 Why This Is Necessary**

Cold email infrastructure is technically fragile in specific and consequential ways. Unauthorized changes to DNS settings, sending volume limits, warm-up intervals, or account configurations can cause rapid and difficult-to-reverse damage to domain reputation and campaign deliverability. Recovery from a damaged sender reputation can take weeks to months and may require abandoning the affected domain entirely.

To protect the Client's $500 investment and give the campaign its best probability of success, ScaleSteady must maintain uninterrupted operational control of the sending environment throughout the engagement. This is a technical requirement, not a business preference.

**8.3 Client Access**

The Client is granted read-level access to campaign dashboards and reporting throughout the engagement. The Client shall not independently alter, reconfigure, or instruct third parties to alter any infrastructure component without ScaleSteady's prior written consent.

**8.4 Termination Releases Operational Control**

Upon termination of services, whether initiated by the Client or by ScaleSteady, all infrastructure assets are transferred to the Client in full and ScaleSteady's operational authority over those assets terminates completely. The Client assumes sole responsibility for the infrastructure from that point forward, and ScaleSteady's service obligations under this Agreement are discharged.

### Section 9 — Post-Milestone Election: Scale or Walk

**9.1 The Election**

Upon achievement of the Revenue Milestone, the Client shall have the exclusive right to elect one of the following two outcomes:

**Option A: Scale (Ongoing Retainer)**

The Client elects to retain ScaleSteady as an ongoing outbound marketing partner at a monthly retainer of **six hundred and ninety-nine dollars (US $699) per month**. The retainer covers continued campaign management, inbox optimization, sequence iteration, and inbox response management as defined in the then-current Scope of Work.

**Option B: Walk (Full Asset Retention)**

The Client elects to conclude the engagement. The Client retains 100% of all infrastructure assets at no further cost: domains, inboxes, lead lists, and email sequences. ScaleSteady does not invoice any labor fee. The Client owes nothing beyond what was paid in the initial Infrastructure Fee.

**9.2 Election Timing**

The Client shall communicate their election in writing within fourteen (14) calendar days of verified Revenue Milestone achievement. Failure to communicate within this window will be treated as an election to Walk (Option B).

**9.3 Retainer Commitment**

Clients who elect Option A enter a **twelve (12)-month retainer commitment** beginning on the first day of the billing cycle following the election. The retainer is billed monthly in advance. The Client may cancel at any time with thirty (30) days' written notice. If the Client cancels before completing twelve (12) months of paid service, an early termination fee equal to three (3) months of the then-current monthly retainer will be assessed and invoiced at the time of cancellation. No further monthly fees will be billed after the early termination fee is paid. ScaleSteady will provide thirty (30) days' advance notice of any retainer pricing change, with any adjustment taking effect no earlier than the next annual renewal.

### Section 10 — Refund Policy and Non-Refundable Purchases

**10.1 General Refund Eligibility**

ScaleSteady operates on a pass-through cost model and defers all labor fees until the Revenue Milestone is achieved. Refund eligibility depends on the stage at which a request is made.

**10.2 Refundable Amounts**

If the Client requests a refund before ScaleSteady executes any vendor purchases on the Client's behalf, the Client is entitled to a **full refund of the Infrastructure Fee**. No work will have commenced and no costs will have been incurred.

**10.3 Non-Refundable Amounts**

Once ScaleSteady has executed vendor purchases on the Client's behalf, including domain registration, inbox provisioning, lead list acquisition, or warm-up platform enrollment, those costs are **non-refundable in whole or in part**. The reasons are straightforward:

(a) ScaleSteady has already disbursed funds to third-party vendors who operate under their own non-refundable policies;

(b) The purchased assets, including domains, inboxes, and lead lists, are transferred to the Client's ownership upon purchase. The Client possesses what was bought; and

(c) ScaleSteady earns no margin on these purchases and therefore has no funds to return.

**10.4 Labor Fee Refunds**

Because ScaleSteady does not collect labor fees during the initial campaign phase, there is no labor fee subject to refund during the Performance Period. If the Client terminates during an active retainer period (Option A), the current month's retainer fee is non-refundable. The early termination fee described in Section 9.3 applies in lieu of any remaining monthly fees.

### Section 11 — Compliance, Indemnification, and Regulatory Responsibility

**11.1 Client Warrants Legal Compliance**

The Client represents and warrants that: (a) their business and its products or services are legally operated in their jurisdiction; (b) the target market and prospect profile provided to ScaleSteady for lead sourcing does not target individuals or entities in jurisdictions where commercial email outreach is prohibited; and (c) the Client will conduct all follow-up communications and sales activities in accordance with applicable law.

**11.2 CAN-SPAM, GDPR, and Equivalent Laws**

Commercial email regulations impose obligations on both the sender and the entity on whose commercial behalf the email is sent. Under the CAN-SPAM Act (United States) and CASL (Canada), primary legal liability attaches to the advertiser — the Client. Under GDPR (European Union), both the data controller (the Client) and the data processor (ScaleSteady) may carry independent compliance obligations. Each party is responsible for meeting the legal requirements applicable to their role. ScaleSteady will implement industry-standard compliance measures within its infrastructure, including functional unsubscribe mechanisms, accurate sender identification, and valid physical address inclusion where required by law. The Client is responsible for ensuring that the target market and content of the campaign comply with the laws of the jurisdictions in which the recipients are located.

**11.3 Client Indemnification**

The Client agrees to indemnify, defend, and hold harmless ScaleSteady and its members, officers, contractors, and agents from and against any claim, loss, liability, fine, or legal expense arising from: (a) the Client's failure to comply with applicable law in their sales or follow-up activities; (b) inaccurate or misleading information provided by the Client for inclusion in campaign materials; or (c) any third-party claim arising from the nature of the Client's products, services, or business practices.

**11.4 Right to Refuse or Suspend**

ScaleSteady reserves the right to pause or permanently discontinue services if, in its reasonable judgment, continued campaign operations would violate applicable regulations, damage ScaleSteady's sender infrastructure, or expose ScaleSteady to material legal or reputational risk. In such cases, ScaleSteady will provide written notice and facilitate asset transfer as prescribed in Section 3.4. No labor fees will be assessed for the period preceding suspension.

### Section 12 — Mutual Cooperation and Client Obligations

**12.1 The Partnership Standard**

The success of an outbound campaign depends on two things: the quality of the infrastructure and outreach that ScaleSteady delivers, and the quality of the sales conversations and follow-through that the Client conducts. ScaleSteady does not operate as a passive service. The Client is expected to engage actively when warm leads arrive, provide accurate business context throughout the campaign, and participate in scheduled reviews. These are not optional courtesies — they are conditions of this partnership.

**12.2 Client Obligations During Campaign**

The Client agrees to:

(a) Respond to warm leads forwarded by ScaleSteady within **two (2) business days** of notification. Delayed responses reduce the probability of conversion from interested prospect to paying client;

(b) Provide accurate business information, offer details, and service descriptions necessary for ScaleSteady to construct campaign messaging that is truthful and compelling;

(c) Notify ScaleSteady of any material change to the Client's offer, pricing, service area, or target audience within five (5) business days of such change, so that campaign materials may be updated accordingly; and

(d) Participate in at least one scheduled campaign review per month during the Performance Period to assess reply quality, adjust angles, and maintain alignment between the outreach and the Client's sales objectives.

**12.3 Client Failure to Cooperate**

If the Client fails to fulfill the obligations in Section 12.2 and that failure materially impairs campaign performance, including missed sales opportunities resulting from unread inbound replies, ScaleSteady shall not be held responsible for any resulting reduction in campaign effectiveness or failure to achieve the Revenue Milestone. ScaleSteady's labor deferral commitment remains in effect; however, non-cooperation will be documented and considered relevant context in any subsequent dispute.

### Section 13 — Limitation of Liability

**13.1 Aggregate Liability Cap**

To the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, ScaleSteady's total aggregate liability to the Client for any claim arising from or related to this Agreement, whether in contract, tort, or otherwise, shall not exceed the total Infrastructure Fee paid by the Client under this Agreement: **five hundred dollars ($500)**. This limitation reflects the nature of ScaleSteady's deferred-fee model. Because no labor fees are collected during the initial campaign phase, there is no larger pool of fees against which a claim could fairly be measured.

**13.2 Exclusion of Consequential Damages**

In no event shall ScaleSteady be liable for indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including lost profits, lost revenue, loss of business opportunity, or reputational harm, even if ScaleSteady has been advised of the possibility of such damages. The Client acknowledges that the commercial outcome of any outbound campaign is subject to market forces and sales variables that are not within ScaleSteady's control.

**13.3 Third-Party Platform Liability**

ScaleSteady is not liable for service interruptions, deliverability failures, data inaccuracies, or policy changes caused by third-party platforms, domain registrars, email service providers, or data vendors. ScaleSteady will take commercially reasonable steps to remediate such issues but cannot guarantee continuity of third-party services.

### Section 14 — Dispute Resolution

**14.1 Good Faith Negotiation**

In the event of any dispute, controversy, or claim arising from or relating to this Agreement, the parties agree to first attempt resolution through direct, good faith negotiation. Either party may initiate this process by delivering written notice describing the nature of the dispute. Both parties will have fifteen (15) calendar days to reach a mutually agreeable resolution before escalating to mediation.

**14.2 Mediation**

If negotiation does not resolve the dispute within fifteen (15) days, the parties agree to submit the matter to non-binding mediation before a mutually agreed-upon mediator. The cost of mediation will be shared equally between the parties.

**14.3 Binding Arbitration**

If mediation does not result in resolution, the dispute shall be submitted to binding arbitration administered by JAMS (Judicial Arbitration and Mediation Services), or by such other arbitration body as both parties agree to in writing at the time the dispute arises. The arbitration shall be conducted in the English language under the applicable JAMS rules in effect at the time of filing. The arbitrator's award shall be final and binding. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction. Nothing in this Section prevents either party from seeking emergency injunctive or equitable relief in a court of competent jurisdiction where necessary to prevent irreparable harm pending arbitration.
