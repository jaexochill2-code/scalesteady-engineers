## 2. How Roofers Actually Get Customers Today

| Rank | Method | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 1 | **Door knocking / canvassing** | Still #1 in 2025. Cost per lead: $3-5 but requires labor, transportation, branded gear. |
| 2 | **Referrals / word of mouth** | Lowest acquisition cost. Highest close rate. Not scalable or controllable. |
| 3 | **Google Local Service Ads (LSAs)** | Pay per verified lead. $150+ CPL in major markets. |
| 4 | **Google Business Profile + Reviews** | Only 28% of roofers use a CRM. Most leave this underdeveloped. |
| 5 | **Direct mail / flyers** | High ROI in storm-affected neighborhoods. |
| 6 | **Yard signs + vehicle wraps** | Neighborhood brand presence. |
| 7 | **Facebook / social ads** | Lower intent. Used for brand awareness and retargeting. |
| 8 | **Storm damage / insurance marketing** | Contractors who respond within 2 hours of a storm capture 40% more leads (Angi 2025). |
| 9 | **Business partnerships** | HVAC companies, gutter installers, real estate agents. |
| 10 | **Networking / community sponsorships** | Youth sports, local events. Trust-building, long-cycle. |

**What is NOT on this list:** Cold email. It is foreign to most residential roofers. That is the opportunity.

## 3. The Language Roofers Use

**Words that earn trust:**
storm damage / storm season / hail / free inspection / free estimate / local / insurance claim / warranty / qualified homeowners / canvassing / door knocking / neighborhood / zip code / your service area

**Words that kill deals:**
campaign / outreach / email marketing / impressions / brand awareness / funnel / strategy / deliverability

**Their actual pain language (from Reddit r/Roofing, r/RoofingSales):**
- "I can't scale canvassing - I can't be everywhere at once"
- "Referrals are great but I have zero control over when they come in"
- "Google Ads cost me $200 per lead and half of them are junk"
- "Storm chasers flood my market and undercut my prices"
- "I need predictable lead flow, not random"
- "I'm paying my guys to walk neighborhoods but half the doors don't answer"

## 8. Bottom Line Assessment

**Is 75,000 emails valuable to roofers?**

Yes - under these conditions:
1. The message is locally relevant (their service zip codes)
2. The offer is what they already pitch door-to-door (free inspection, storm damage)
3. The language is plain - no marketing-speak
4. The pitch is framed as "digital canvassing" not "email marketing"
5. The storm trigger angle is included as the premium differentiation

**The risk:** Residential homeowner email conversion is inherently lower because roofing is infrequent-purchase and trust-dependent. The floor concept is the honest framing. Do not oversell conversion rate - sell reach and brand presence at a cost that makes canvassing look expensive.
